EXAMINATION AND PROMOTION
  1. There are two examinations (Half yearly and Final) besides two Unit Tests (VIII to X) and Two Terminal Exams. (KG-I to VII)
  2. Promotions are granted on the basis of the whole year’s work.
  3. Pupil’s absence-for an examination for any reason is not re-examined. In case of illness Doctor’s Certificate must be produced.
  4. Progress Report should be collected and signed by the Parents and it should be returned within five days of issue.
  5. The result declared at the end of the year is final and not reconsidered.
  6. Pupil’s whose dues are not cleared completely will be debarred from sitting for examinations.
GENERAL INFORMATION
  1. Boys and girls of age 4 and above are admitted from L.K.G onward.
  2. Every pupil should possess a copy of the Diary and must bring it to class daily.
  3. Pupil must be in the School Campus in proper school uniform at least 10 (ten) minutes before the class time.
  4. English is the common language allowed in the school.
  5. Foul language and conduct and disobedience to the school authority will lead to dismissal.
  6. Pupils are not allowed to leave the school premises during school hours without the permission of the Principal / Headmistress.
  7. Perfect silence must be observed during and after the assembly.
  8. Cleanliness and tidiness are expected from all.
  9. Students are solely responsible for their personal belongings, books, writing materials, tiffin boxes, etc.
  10. The school is not responsible for the loss of articles or money. It is advisable not to bring valuable articles to the school.
  11. In the absence of the Teacher, the Class Captain is responsible for maintaining discipline.
GENERAL INFORMATION

This is an English Medium Co-educational Institution (Private) established in 1994. It is open to all good and earnest students irrespective of religion or community.

We aim at imparting a sound education by a life of discipline in obedience and truth, seeking wisdom and knowledge, trying to develop a scientific temper and cultivate the aesthetic sense through artistic creation and cultural programmes, imbibing a sense of duty to themselves, the community, and the state, and thus to equip the students well for future studies.

SCHOOL FEES
  1. School fee are paid for 12 (twelve) months.
  2. Annual fees shall be paid together with the tuition fees of the first month in the new Academic Session.
  3. No student is allowed to sit for the Half Yearly & Final Examination unless all fees are paid up to the date.
  4. Fees once paid are not refundable.
  5. Fees are to be paid before the 10th of the every month, failing which a fine of Rs.50/- will be charged.
  6. Pupils joining or leaving the School during any term will have to pay all the fees for the full term including the annual fees.
  7. The School fees & other dues are liable to be revised at any time.
ADMISSION AND WITHDRAWAL
  1. A pupil who joins fresh has to produce an official Birth Certificate such as Municipal, Baptismal, vaccination or hospital record in support of the birth date entered in the admission form.
  2. Transfer Certificate will be issued when all dues have been paid in full and on receipt of a written application from the parents / guardians mentioning the name, class, section, roll number and admission number of their ward.
  3. Pupils coming from the other schools must produce:
    • Transfer - cum - Character Certificate
    • Original Mark Sheet of last Examination.
    • Other Certificate of Merit (if any)
  4. New students must be introduced personally by their parents / guardians, who will be responsible for their regularity and fees.
  5. A fee of Rs. 500/- will be charged for the transfer certificate. If a duplicate copy is required later a fee of Rs. 500/- will be charged.
TO PARENTS
  1. Take personal interest in the progress of your child and help him/her in all round development.
  2. Meet his/her teacher regularly and co-operate with them in educating your child.
  3. Study the school regulations carefully and follow them faithfully.
  4. Check the school diary and sign the notes if any sent by the Principal/Teacher.
  5. Send your child on time and take him/her back immediately after school hours. Guide him/her to be independent, resourceful and useful member of the society. Help him/her to do what he/she can himself/herself do. Include them in the dignity of labour and respect for all human beings.
  6. Parents should not walk on the Varandahs, or enter the classrooms or interview the teachers during the working hours without the permission of the Principal.
  7. Parents may kindly understand that a student in whose presence his teacher and school are criticized loses his respect to his teacher and fails to learn from the teacher consequently.
  8. Parents are earnestly requested to see that their children attend school regularly, are punctual, prepare their lessons and do their homework assigned to them.
